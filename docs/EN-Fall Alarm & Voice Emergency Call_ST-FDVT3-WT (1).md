## **1. Product Introduction** 

The ST-FDVT3-WT fall alarm & Voice Emergency Call radar sensor is a product designed to monitor human spatial behavioral using millimeter wave radar technology. It is capable of detecting the presence of human body and providing fall alarm functionality without physical contact. Additionally, it supports Voice Emergency Call and provides night light illumination. Through Wi-Fi network, it can be linked to the Tuya cloud platform and controlled by the App of Tuya Smart for scene linkage and status reporting.

## **2. Installation Method** 

## **2.1 Radar detection range** 

Horizontal wide beam angle: 100 °, horizontal narrow beam angle: 90 °. 

Human presence detection range: approx. 4 m × 3 m. 

Static dwell alert and fall detection range: approx. 3 m × 2.5 m. 

The detection area is an elliptical region; the direction parallel to the function button is the major axis of the ellipse. 

## **2.2 Installation Position Recommendations** 

The device needs to be installed horizontally at the top. The installation height range is 2.2-2.8m, with a recommended installation height of 2.4m. The radar should be securely installed directly above the detection area, covering the main human activity area.

## **2.3 Installation Precautions** 

The radar is sensitive to slight movements within the space. During detection, it's important to avoid vibrations. When installing, try to avoid placing the device near items such as exhaust hoods, fans, flowing water, and vents. This radar detects abnormal body postures and sudden movements caused by falls. It will trigger a fall alarm after the preset delay (minimum 5 seconds) once a fall posture is identified.

## **5.3 Description of functions** 

## **5.3.1 Occupied** 

Indicates that there is someone in the current detection environment. The response time for triggering the presence of someone is less than 300ms, which is more sensitive than PIR infrared detection. 

## **5.3.2 No Presence / Vacant** 

It means there is no one in the current detection environment. The device will continue to determine whether there is still micro-motion in the area. When it is determined that it is unoccupied for more than 30 seconds, the device will output the No Presence / Vacant status. 

* If the device has been unable to enter unoccupied status, it is recommended to check whether there is micro-motion interference in the space, such as fluttering curtains, moving sweeping robots, or animals. 

## **5.3.3 Active** 

It means that someone is moving in the current detection environment, and the radar will continue to detect the movement of people 

## **5.3.4 Static** 

Represents the status of someone standing, sitting or lying quietly in the current detection environment. The radar will continue to detect changes in the status of the person and report it. 

## **5.3.5 Alert status** 

1）No alarm status currently: Nobody fall down 2） 

Fall alarm: Someone has fallen down 

## *** If someone falls in the detection environment, the fall status will continue until the person gets up safely.** 

3) If someone is still in the detection environment for a long time (fainting, lying down or sitting still), the stationary dwell state and dwell alarm will be displayed. The dwell alarm will remain until the person recovers.
